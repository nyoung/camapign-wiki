# Were-Rabbit

## Description
A bipedal lycanthrope standing seven to eight feet tall to the tips of its ears. Massive claws for front paws. Scars crisscrossing its back and sides — evidence of prior battles or a hard life. Agile enough to leap silently across rooftops and burrow rapidly under walls. Can command or direct swarms of ordinary rabbits as a vanguard.

[[Boris Dorchen]] had previously claimed to have hunted one in the Drakwald — this was the subject of considerable mockery. The encounter at [[Fox and Crown]] confirmed were-rabbits are entirely real.

## Traits
- **Claws** — massive, capable of a devastating leaping charge attack
- **Leap** — crosses rooftops in a single bound; lands with a thunderous thump
- **Burrow** — digs under walls and doors with extreme speed
- **Swarm Vanguard** — directs or attracts swarms of ordinary rabbits to overwhelm multiple targets simultaneously

## Encounter Notes — Fox and Crown (21 Nachexen 2512)

**Outcome: Killed by [[Boris Dorchen]]. See [[Session 07 - The Were-Rabbit's End]].**

- Rabbit swarms emerged first: dozens to hundreds of small rabbits burrowing under the south gate and through holes in the east wall
- The were-rabbit appeared on the rooftop above [[Joerg Wusterburg]]'s position; leaped to the kitchen roof; launched a diving charge at Joerg — claws slashed his leg and knocked him back with tremendous force
- Performed a supernatural leap from the compound to the second-floor balcony, landing adjacent to [[Boris Dorchen]]
- [[Hildegarde]] landed a **critical hit** on the back leg — creature fell prone and stunned
- While prone, [[Boris Dorchen]] dropped his crossbow, drew his axe, and decapitated it with a single overhand swing
- **Upon death:** the creature — head, severed foot, and torso — immediately shrank back to the size of a normal black rabbit; lycanthrope form entirely reverted
- **Effect on swarms:** all remaining rabbits transformed from rabid aggressors into timid wild rabbits and fled into the darkness

## Weaknesses / Tactics
- Highly mobile — does not stay in one place; use rooftops and tunnels to reposition
- Swarms used as distraction/area denial while it manoeuvres
- Prefers ambush attacks; leaping charge is devastating
- Scars suggest it has survived multiple serious injuries — resilient
- **Vulnerability:** when brought prone and stunned, loses all mobility advantage; standard decapitation effective
