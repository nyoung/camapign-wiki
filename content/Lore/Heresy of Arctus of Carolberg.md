# Heresy of Arctus of Carolberg

## Summary
A condemned heresy, declared anathema in 1265. Arctus of Carolberg denied Sigmar's noble birth and rank, thereby undermining the divine sanction of noble rule. To assert that Sigmar was not of noble birth — not a graf or nobleman — is to commit this heresy.

## Full Details
The [[Ordo Sancti Inquisita]] applied this doctrine to [[Klaus Grebel]]'s street speech, arguing he had proclaimed Sigmar was not noble-born, thus delegitimising the divine foundation of nobility itself.

[[Judge Magdalyn Verner]]'s verdict noted that Klaus's rhetoric about guilds and economic injustice suggested he was invoking Sigmar as an example against guild cartels — using Sigmar's humble origins rhetorically rather than doctrinally — which mitigated the full severity of the charge.

## Implications / Party Relevance
- The [[Ordo Sancti Inquisita]] can use this doctrine against any speaker who invokes Sigmar's origins in a populist or anti-noble context
- [[Klaus Grebel]] was found guilty under this heresy but received a lenient sentence
- The doctrine has political utility: it can be weaponised to silence economic or class-based criticism if framed in religious terms
