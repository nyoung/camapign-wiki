# Boris

## Description
Boris is a young man with a hunter's bearing, raised beneath the dark canopy of the Drakwald forest. He carries himself with the quiet confidence of someone accustomed to moving through dangerous terrain — watchful, direct, and comfortable with violence. When he introduced himself to [[Falconius Thorncobble]], his words were simple and entirely characteristic: "a young hunter from Middenland." He wears an Ulrican pendant that he uses as a fist-load in a brawl and retrieves from a beer mug without a word.

## Personality
Boris is fiercely, almost viscerally devout to Ulric. His father told him that Sigmar was an Ulrican, and he carries that conviction like a weapon — the mere suggestion that he might be a Sigmarite makes his jaw tighten. He does not hate quietly. He moves toward danger, not away from it, and his instincts in a fight are impressively improvisational: he has used a rat-laden pole as a flail, a man as a human shield, and finished the [[Were-Rabbit]] with a single decapitating axe swing after [[Hildegarde]] left it prone. He is brave and blunt, but he is not calculating — he once tried to pocket all five reward coins while the entire party watched, a coin fell and rolled, and he scrambled after it on the floor.

He is earnest where others are guarded, curious where others are indifferent. He wondered aloud whether a ruined elf-human temple might be part of Athel Loren. He worries about his reputation as a were-rabbit hunter and works to cultivate it, but his competitive streak is genuine rather than vain — he gave [[Hildegarde]] the rabbit's foot because she had earned it alongside him. He is uncomfortable in open country; growing up under the Drakwald canopy left him with a genuine agoraphobia when the forest thinned on the Nordland road, the widening sky feeling wrong in a way he could not quite name. He found Elke Grebel's charity natural and unsurprising — village life in the Drakwald taught him that kind of redistribution.

He is socially clumsy and cannot reliably count, but nobody questions his competence in the field.

## Background
Comes from the Drakwald forests, where he hunted dangerous creatures. His father had a saying: "Sigmar was an Ulrican" — a conviction Boris has carried into every theological argument since. His reputation as a were-rabbit hunter was long a source of mockery; that reputation is now vindicated in blood. He has since made the were-rabbit his identity, collecting furs for a hat and warm boots, distributing trophies, and asking those who witnessed the battle to spread the story. He describes himself simply as "a young hunter from Middenland," which is true and also, increasingly, an understatement.
