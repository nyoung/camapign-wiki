# Pilpoul

## Description
Pilpoul is an agitator, autodidact, and self-described rhetorical force of nature. He describes himself at warrant offices as an "autodidact" — because it is the most impressive word available that is also technically true. He is compact in his ambitions only insofar as he has not yet acquired a printing press.

## Personality
Pilpoul's entire worldview was forged at sixteen, when corrupt tax collectors ruined his family in Altdorf's cooperage district. Everything that followed — the legal self-education, the street oratory, the relentless agitation — flows directly from that wound. He cites Article 12 of the Codex Civis Middenheimenis in court, knows the Rule of Pre-Trial Disclosure, and effectively won Klaus Grebel's case through eloquent cross-examination and an intent-based defence that reduced the gallery to murmuring against the judge. He was fined 5s 6d for grandstanding and considered it worth it.

He delivers speeches everywhere: on crates, from windows, at taverns, into the faces of enemies mid-brawl. He reframed the exit tax at Beekerhoven's town square as a sign of opportunity — and was immediately deflated by a farmer noting nobody pays it anyway. He takes this in stride. He is inventive with cover stories: [[Hildegarde]] has been his biblically-married wife (with five children) and a woman gravely ill with bursting boils, depending on the door. He told the half-delirious, near-dead Hildegarde after the Forest Inn that he would have let her die, but she would not die in vain. He was not joking, exactly, but he was not being cruel either. He considers this honest.

He wants to fund a printing operation. He made a religious sign to Shallya after the trial, hoping it made them even regarding his lost gold crown. He refuses to compromise on his beauty rest.

## Background
Grew up in Altdorf's cooperage district. His family was ruined by corrupt tax collectors when he was sixteen — the defining catastrophe of his life, the engine of his career. He is self-educated in law, rhetoric, and agitation, and describes himself, accurately, as an "autodidact." He came to Middenheim as a pamphleteer and street agitator and was mid-speech near the city wall when the sewer attack interrupted him. He is not the sort of person whose speeches get interrupted without consequences for everyone involved.
