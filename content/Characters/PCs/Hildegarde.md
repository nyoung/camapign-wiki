# Hildegarde

## Description
Hildegarde is a tall, gaunt woman with prematurely white-blonde hair and mismatched eyes — one grey, one blue. The hair turned white from years of street hardship, not age; she is not old. She carries herself with the wiry alertness of someone accustomed to reading a room from a doorway. She wears well-worn but decent clothing, a marked improvement from the rags she arrived in, provided by the [[Temple of Shallya]]. She currently carries the were-rabbit's severed foot as a trophy, which she received from [[Boris Dorchen]] and raised above her head like an excited child.

## Personality
Hildegarde has strong moral instincts that she wears lightly, deflecting any personal question with "just someone who helps as one should." The deflection is genuine — she does not know her own history, and the question of her mother is the one wound she cannot leave alone. She gave up her only memento, a brass necklace, to [[Lady Steiner]] in exchange for an investigation into her missing mother, and said nothing to anyone about what it cost her.

She has brave impulses that tip regularly into recklessness. She was shot twice outside the Forest Inn, fell prone and bleeding, and crawled forward through sheer will. Neck deep in a near-fatal wound, barely conscious, her only question was whether Hoffman had been saved. She bid everything she had at the wife sale to outbid for [[Karyssa Bucer]]'s freedom, lost, and stormed out furious. When Karyssa's former husband made a joke about marrying her instead, Hildegarde slapped him. She climbed a table at Skralberg to end a brawl and tried the same tactic during the Middenheim plaza riot — a recurring instinct to de-escalate by making herself visible and loudly claiming shared ground.

She is secretly self-doubting about her worth and her place. She fumbles under social pressure, held the warrant upside down at the Collegium, and becomes nervous when flustered. But she is street-smart and effective when it counts: she climbed a second-story window while the others distracted at the front door, posed repeatedly as "the professor's abandoned wife" with effective results, and used subtle intimidation to get the Commission clerk to pay out the reward.

She also believes, wholeheartedly, that she is a magnificent singer. She is not.

## Background
Hildegarde has lived on the streets of Middenheim all her life. She knows no other history. As a child, her father threatened to send her to the Middenheim mining colony — the shape of her upbringing, in brief. She has street contacts throughout the city, knows the sewers are more dangerous now (fewer rat catchers, more dwarf patrols), and was stunned the first time she witnessed Elke Grebel's charitable redistribution, having never seen such generosity given freely. Her missing mother remains the central unresolved question of her life. The only memento she had was a brass necklace. It is gone now, in [[Lady Steiner]]'s hands, and she is waiting for word.
