# Anton Petz

## Description
Middle-aged proprietor of the [[Pewter Merchant]] coaching inn on the [[Ostland]] coast road. Presents a clean apron and a well-kept courtyard — but his hands never stop twisting and untwisting the corners of a towel, and his eyes keep drifting toward a particular spot near the hearth. A man whose composure is being maintained through sheer effort.

## Role / Occupation
Innkeeper and proprietor of the [[Pewter Merchant]]. Serves travellers on the road between the [[Royal Wolf Inn]] and [[Ferlangen]].

## Personality
Anxious, decent, trapped. Terrified of losing his inn to the Ostland law that levies a hundred-crown fine on any location where a noble is slain — nearly as terrified as he is of witch hunters. Willingly informed the party of [[Christina Asper]]'s presence at the inn the night of the killing, but his fear of her is obvious.

## Relationships
- Doesn't want the attention of [[Christina Asper]] but was willing to name her as a witness
- Eager to trust the party if they can help him

## History with the Party
Met in [[Session 17 - Asper-ated|Session 17]]. Allowed the party into the storeroom to examine the body. Provided key intelligence:

- Several days prior, a fight broke out between a "noble" and a group of **reivers** from the north, resulting in the noble's death
- Under Ostland law, a noble slain on his premises means a **hundred-crown fine** — ruinous
- The dead man had arrived on horseback with **no luggage**, presenting himself as the merchant **[[Masten Feltt]]**
- A **distinguished gentleman** matching [[Professor Udo Stradovski|Stradovski]]'s wanted-poster description had passed through 12–14 days earlier, paying with a **freshly minted gold crown** bearing suspicious markings — travelled alone, stayed one night, ate a meal, departed at first light without a word about his destination
- [[Christina Asper]] was present at the inn the night of the killing

[[Hildegarde]] pressed a shilling into his hand as goodwill — a gesture both of them knew barely dented the hundred-crown problem.

## Known Information
- Owner of the [[Pewter Merchant]]
- Under immediate threat of ruin from Ostland noble-death law
- Witnessed the arrival and killing of the man pretending to be the merchant [[Masten Feltt]]
- Kept the illegal gold crown Stradovski paid with — surrendered it to the party
- Knows Asper was there the night of the killing

## Secrets / GM Notes
- The dead man was **not** a noble — a commoner in ill-fitting clothes. The PDF implies the actual killed man is [[Werner Herst]] (a servant), and [[Masten Feltt]] is the real merchant whose identity he had assumed. The party has deduced the commoner-impostor angle but has not yet reached the servant/real-merchant layer
- A successful appeal to an Imperial road warden or to the coroner can likely rescind the fine
