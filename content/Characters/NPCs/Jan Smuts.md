# Jan Smuts

## Description
Proprietor of the [[Hemp Merchant]] coaching inn at the junction of the Ostland coast road and the track running north. When the party arrived, he was run ragged — the inn swarming with **Imperialist Engineer Pioneer Corps** soldiers and [[Landsturm]] — and barely had time to breathe between tables.

## Role / Occupation
Innkeeper. Handling a coaching inn under military occupation.

## Personality
Overwhelmed but cooperative. After the soldiers retired for the night he spoke plainly with the party — confirming what he had seen, and, when the [[Pewter Merchant]] murder victim's birthmark was described to him, he went pale and identified the body as his own missing servant boy.

## Relationships
- His **servant boy** — missing three or four days; now confirmed to be the Pewter Merchant corpse
- **[[Albrecht Felt]]** — a merchant who stayed at the inn; vanished at the same time as the servant boy; the boy was last seen **helping him onto his horse**
- [[Professor Udo Stradovski]] — stayed at the inn the night of the 16th Nachexen
- [[Elfrieda Teuschel]] — stayed two nights ago with one burly male companion, paid with a freshly minted crown

## History with the Party
**Session 18:** Cornered by the party after the soldiers retired. Confirmed:
- [[Professor Udo Stradovski|Stradovski]] matched the description of a guest who stayed the night of the **16th Nachexen**; travelled alone on horseback; haughty Middenland accent
- [[Elfrieda Teuschel]]'s group passed through **two nights prior** — dark-haired noblewoman with a large, burly male servant, paying with a **freshly minted crown** matching the illegal currency the party has been tracking
- When [[Boris Dorchen|Boris]] described the **birthmark** of the murder victim at the [[Pewter Merchant]], Smuts went pale: the mark belonged to **his own servant boy**, vanished three or four days ago with the merchant **[[Albrecht Felt]]**

He was too overwhelmed to send anyone west to identify the body; [[Pilpoul Ivri|Pilpoul]] offered to help draft a **letter** to be carried back by the next westbound traveller.
