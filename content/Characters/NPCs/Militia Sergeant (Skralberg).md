# Militia Sergeant (Skralberg)

## Description
Sergeant of the Nordland militia at the [[Skralberg]] border post. Commands the crossing with the default suspicion of a man who has seen bad things come through that road and knows it.

## Role / Occupation
Nordland militia sergeant commanding the [[Skralberg]] border post at the Nordland-Ostland border.

## Personality
Suspicious but fair. He accused the party of being Ostland spies or plague carriers before they had said much — a working assumption at this border, apparently, not personal malice. Once [[Berin]] named the Drakwald Hunters and [[Hildegarde]] produced the Imperial warrant, the hostility stepped down and he became cooperative. He ended the brawl involving [[Volmar]] cleanly when [[Hildegarde]] made her table-speech — he knows when to call his men off.

He is well-informed about local conditions and shared useful intelligence without prompting: [[Professor Udo Stradovski|Stradovski]]'s passage, the Eichenhof road barricade (which quieted the whole table when [[Berin]] raised it), and the recent Ostland raider attacks. He is a functional local authority doing a job on a difficult border, and he does it competently.

## History with the Party

**Session 16 (Skralberg border post):** Initially barred the party's entry, accusing them of being Ostland spies or plague carriers. Allowed them through after [[Berin]] named the Drakwald Hunters and [[Hildegarde]] showed the Imperial warrant.

After the brawl involving [[Volmar]], shared crucial intelligence: [[Professor Udo Stradovski]] had passed through Skralberg approximately a week and a half before Session 16, carrying dark academic texts and a physician's bag.

When [[Berin]] mentioned Eichenhof, the entire table went quiet. The sergeant explained that the road to Eichenhof was barricaded — only plague bearers were directed that way — and that Stradovski had taken the road east into Ostland instead.

Also reported that Ostland raiders had struck a nearby farm in recent days, stealing livestock and abducting a young farm girl.
