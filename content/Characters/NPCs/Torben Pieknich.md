# Torben Pieknich

## Description
Presented himself as the local toll keeper and head of the inn's defenses at the [[Forest Inn]]. In reality a road warden who had betrayed his Imperial oath to serve [[Melmoth]]'s ghoul network — kept in his human role, like [[Van Moll]], to gather intelligence from arriving travellers and maintain the facade of a legitimate waystation. He is currently a prisoner with a shattered tibia.

## Role / Occupation
False road warden and intelligence operative for [[Melmoth]]'s ghoul network at the [[Forest Inn]]. Betrayed his Imperial oath; fired the crossbow bolt that nearly killed [[Hildegarde]].

## Personality
Torben is a capable deceiver in low-stakes situations. He inserted himself smoothly into a conversation [[Berin]] was having, presenting himself as a helpful local with useful knowledge about the road north — and he was believed. He watched [[Condradine]] with [[Hoffman Jannitzer]] and tracked the party's movements without drawing attention.

Under sustained pressure after the battle, that composure broke. [[Berin]] cornered him and he confessed fully: Stradovski's visit, what the professor had done, what Melmoth had accepted in exchange. He folded quickly once the game was clearly over. The crossbow bolt he fired at [[Hildegarde]] was not the act of a true believer — it was the act of a man who had gone too far to back out and chose the worst available option.

## History with the Party

**Session 13 (Forest Inn):** Confirmed that three riders matching [[Elfrieda Teuschel]]'s group had skirted the toll two nights prior and headed northeast on the Erengrad Road toward Seuchenshof. Warned ominously about that destination. [[Pilpoul]] noticed him watching [[Condradine]] with [[Hoffman Jannitzer]] and watched him back.

**Session 15 (battle and aftermath):** Revealed to have been posing as a road warden while actually serving [[Melmoth]]. Captured and bound after the battle.

**Session 16 (prisoner):** Held as a prisoner with a shattered tibia. The party debated his fate and decided to transport him to the first proper authority they encountered.
