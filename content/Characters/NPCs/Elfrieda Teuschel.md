# Elfrieda Teuschel

## Description
Raven-haired and noticeably younger than her husband [[Manfred Teuschel]], [[Elfrieda Teuschel|Elfrieda]] lives — or lived — in the large house with pillars on Schlosse de Vies Street in [[Beekerhoven]]. The home presents well: good woodwork, paintings, a respectable address. [[Berin Garibaldi]] noticed some of the furniture was cheaper than it appeared, imitations placed for effect. The impression the house gives is of someone who understands what wealth looks like and has arranged the signifiers with care.

When the party visited, she was poised and well-dressed, her answers measured and deliberate. She was last seen departing by black coach with a raven-haired companion and a broad-shouldered driver, heading north through road conditions her coachman clearly didn't respect — and then overland on horseback after the wheel shattered, because she didn't stop.

## Role / Occupation
Former wife of [[Manfred Teuschel]], the [[Beekerhoven]] minter — now a wanted fugitive. The true scope of her role has expanded considerably: she was not simply a grateful former patient of [[Professor Udo Stradovski]]'s but an active architect of his escape network. She arranged the false Salzenmund trail via [[Marx Khlesl]], provided freshly minted coins from the mint as travel funds, organised supplies through the [[Medical Union]], and — when the time came — took a large shipment of stolen mint gold and vanished north ahead of the party.

## Personality
Elfrieda is controlled in a way that reads as practiced. When [[Berin Garibaldi|Berin]] and [[Hildegarde]] visited, her answers were given carefully — confirming just enough, volunteering nothing. When the suggestion of dark magic came up, a flash of genuine anger crossed her face before she composed herself and redirected to the miracle of Shallya. That moment of anger is notable: it felt less like denial and more like personal affront, as if the framing offended something she believed in.

She was visibly displeased by [[Manfred Teuschel|Elson]]'s impulse to give alms — and the party had barely left when he remarked on her displeasure. Whatever she's protecting, she has been protecting it carefully, and long enough that her husband's casually generous nature has become a liability she measures against.

The woman who fled was not panicking. She packed her documents and jewelry, took the gold, and left two open lockboxes for [[Manfred Teuschel|Elson]] to find. She has abandoned her marriage and her established life in [[Beekerhoven]] for something she considers worth more. What she believes [[Professor Udo Stradovski|Stradovski]] is going to do in [[Ferlangen]] — and what she was cured of, and how — may be the same answer.

## History with the Party

**Session 08:** [[Berin Garibaldi]] and [[Hildegarde]] visited. Elfrieda confirmed Stradovski treated her for a wasting disease years ago in [[Middenheim]], returning in amazing health — stronger than ever. Denied dark magic. Revealed Stradovski visited c. 11 Nachexen 2512, stayed one night, and left. Berin observed micro-expressions indicating she was hiding something.

**Session 10:** Falconius discovered a note in the [[Medical Union Compound]] addressed to Marx Khlesl, signed "E. Teuschel," instructing him to prepare provisions for the professor for a journey to **Ferlangen** — confirming she was actively organising the operation.

**Session 11:** After the Medical Union raid, the Verenans noted a recent scandal where coins went missing from the mint around the time Elfrieda received treatment from the professor — a new connection. Manfred Teuschel reported that Elfrieda had **gone missing** with a mousy-haired waif in an artisan's smock and a large shipment of gold from the mint. Hildegarde searched the bedroom — found two open lockboxes (one empty, one with freshly minted pennies), disarrayed dressing table, missing jewelry. Elfrieda had taken important documents and her valuables.

**Session 12:** Town crier announced Elfrieda "Toishaw" (her alias or clerical variation) was now officially **wanted** by the Beekerhoven authorities for involvement in the corpse desecration plot. All newly minted coins declared stolen property. A black coach matching her description passed through the Hunter's Lodge the night before the party arrived — driven by a broad-shouldered man, accompanied by a raven-haired woman (her). They continued north despite road warnings.

**Session 12 (later):** The party found her **crashed coach** on the road — one wheel shattered, dead horse with broken leg (put down). Inside: a trunk of women's clothing her size. Three horses continuing north; Connie identified a distinctive proud nail in one horseshoe. Elfrieda and companion abandoned the coach and rode on toward Ferlangen.

**Session 16:** Two women and a brutish man passed through Schoten (Ostland) three nights before Session 16 — likely Elfrieda's group. She is still ahead of the party.

**Session 18:** Confirmed at the [[Hemp Merchant]] by [[Jan Smuts]] — a dark-haired noblewoman with **one large, burly male companion**, staying **two nights ago** and paying with a **freshly minted crown** matching the illegal currency the party has been tracking. Her lead over the party has now closed to **~2 days**.
