# William Coin

## Description
Servant of [[Elfrieda Teuschel]] and [[Manfred Teuschel]], in their home on Schlosse de Vies Street. Not a loyalist — more a functionary doing a job.

## Personality
Cooperative when given sufficient incentive. After the party's initial visit concluded, [[Hildegarde]] bribed him with ten shillings and he provided useful information without apparent hesitation or guilt. He is not a zealot for his employers; he simply does what servants do, including talking when the pay is right.

## History with the Party
- [[Session 08 - The Minter's Wife]]: Answered the door when [[Berin Garibaldi]] and [[Hildegarde]] called. [[Berin]]'s charm convinced him to fetch his masters. After the party's visit concluded, [[Hildegarde]] bribed him with ten shillings for additional information. He revealed: (1) [[Professor Udo Stradovski]] had been seeking a **mount to purchase**; (2) the professor had considerable travel gear — **lanterns and a tent** — suggesting an overland journey; (3) William directed him to the **town stables**, the only place in Beekerhoven to buy a mount.

## Known Information
- Stradovski was looking for a horse to buy during his visit (~11 Nachexen 2512)
- Stradovski had lanterns and a tent with him — overland travel planned
- William directed him to the Beekerhoven town stables
