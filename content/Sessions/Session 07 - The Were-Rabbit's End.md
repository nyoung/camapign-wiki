# Session 07 — The Were-Rabbit's End

**Played:** 10 January 2026
**In-game date:** 21–23 Nachexen 2512

## Summary

The combat against the [[Were-Rabbit]] and its rabbit swarms concluded with a decisive victory. [[Boris Dorchen]] beheaded the prone creature with his axe, triggering an instant transformation back to ordinary rabbit form. The party collected their reward from innkeeper [[Emma Ludaroth]], rested at the [[Fox and Crown]], and then continued north. [[Joerg Wusterburg]] departed the group to return to [[Middenheim]] and report the chaos beast to his temple. After an overnight at [[Ulric's Vision]], the party arrived at [[Beekerhoven]] by midday on 23 Nachexen.

## Key Events

### Conclusion of the Were-Rabbit Battle (21 Nachexen, night)

- [[Hoffman Janitzer]] rushed from the kitchen, switching to hand weapon to support the party
- [[Boris Dorchen]] scored a crossbow hit on the were-rabbit — superficial
- [[Joerg Wusterburg]] manoeuvred to avoid being flanked, attacked with dagger — minimal damage
- [[Pilpoul Ivri]] missed with sling, then landed a second shot on the were-rabbit
- The were-rabbit slashed Joerg's leg and knocked him back with tremendous force; then performed a supernatural leap onto the second-floor balcony, landing adjacent to Boris
- [[Erika Sÿfrid von Drakkenheim]] swung at a bunny swarm — the rabbits dodged
- Hoffman switched to sword and shield and obliterated a bunny swarm entirely — rabbits scattered in all directions
- Boris disengaged from the were-rabbit and repositioned; crossbow shot struck the creature
- Joerg attacked a swarm — damaged but did not destroy it
- Pilpoul's sling stone hit another swarm; the wounded rabbits faltered and fled into the darkness
- [[Hildegarde]] fired her crossbow at the were-rabbit — **critical hit**, piercing deep into its back leg; the creature fell prone and was stunned
- Bunny swarms continued assaulting the party: Erika took a hit to her leg; Joerg was bitten again, left badly wounded
- Erika attempted to flip a produce cart to trap the swarms — strained a muscle in the attempt before the cart moved; Hoffman moved to assist for future attempts
- The were-rabbit recovered from its stunned state and hauled itself upright using the railing, balancing on one leg
- Boris dropped his crossbow, drew his axe, and charged the prone creature — **killing blow**: a clean overhand swing severed the were-rabbit's head completely
- Instant transformation: the were-rabbit — head, severed foot, and torso — shrank back to the size of a normal black rabbit
- Effect on the swarms: all remaining rabbits transformed from rabid aggressors into timid wild rabbits, fleeing in every direction

### Aftermath and Reward

- Boris gathered approximately twenty dead rabbits onto his cloak as proof
- Pilpoul fetched water and buckets to tend to the wounded and clean the carcasses
- The party returned to the main inn; evening patrons still present
- Boris presented the dead rabbits and the severed were-rabbit head to [[Emma Ludaroth]], the innkeeper
- Boris and Hildegarde addressed the patrons; Hildegarde backed up Boris's account of events
- Emma confirmed the produce was intact and paid **5 Gold Crowns** as promised
- Boris attempted to pocket all five coins; one fell and rolled, forcing him to chase it in front of the entire party — he eventually agreed to distribute one coin to each party member
- Party received complimentary ale, rabbit stew, and rooms with rabbit fur blankets
- Boris planned to collect enough rabbit fur for a hat and warm boots
- Hildegarde asked Boris for the were-rabbit's foot as a trophy; he agreed — she lifted it above her head with obvious delight
- The next morning, Hildegarde woke early and asked Emma to spread the tale of the were-rabbits to enhance Boris's reputation as a rabbit hunter

### Joerg's Departure (22 Nachexen)

- Joerg announced his intention to leave the party and return to [[Middenheim]] to report the chaos beast to his [[Order of the Howling Wolf]] temple
- The party bid him farewell; Hildegarde encouraged him to stay in touch

### The Road North (22–23 Nachexen)

- Journey through the forest was uneventful — no beastmen or attacks
- [[Mungo Mudfoot]] regaled the party with stories of his ancestors, including his great elder halfling ancestor accidentally tripping Magnus the Pious
- Mungo tied the reins to the post, climbed into the back of the moving wagon, and returned with mugs of ale for the riders — apparently the lead horse knew to follow the wagon in front
- The party spotted a side road heading east — Mungo or a companion noted it led to the Middenheim mining colony, where condemned prisoners work off their debts; Hildegarde recalled her father threatening to send her there as a child

### Ulric's Vision (night of 22 Nachexen)

- Arrived at [[Ulric's Vision]], an inn deep in the trees, marked by a faded wooden sign with a crude eye carving
- [[Jan Gerster]], the stout innkeeper, greeted them and reported that eight knights (a mix of Sigmarites and Ulricans, apparently antagonistic toward each other) had departed the previous night — confirmed as the Inquisitors and Ulrican knights the party is pursuing
- Secured a four-person room for the night
- Farm workers in the common room asked for news from Middenheim
- Hildegarde, emboldened by ale, stood on her chair and attempted to sing a song about the were-rabbit battle — performed very badly (off-key, nonsensical lyrics, poor rhyming)
- Pilpoul stepped in and delivered a speech about common people rising up against tyranny (the were-rabbit standing in); the entertainment-starved crowd was reasonably entertained
- Hildegarde believed the crowd loved her performance

### Arrival at Beekerhoven (23 Nachexen)

- Departed Ulric's Vision in warm weather with light rain
- By midday: passed through cut fields and small villages before reaching [[Beekerhoven]]'s ancient stone walls — crumbling mortar and weathered stonework, but the gatehouse featured thick new stonework and defensive additions
- Guards waved the party through without demanding coin at entry
- A weathered sign at the gate proclaimed exit fees for all departing travellers: **2 shillings per leg, 4 shillings per wheel**
- Mungo announced he would remain at the caravan fortification outside the city to avoid the exit fee
- Boris asked a guard about the unusual charge and confirmed the fee structure
- The party discussed removing supplies from the cart before entry to avoid paying exit fees later
