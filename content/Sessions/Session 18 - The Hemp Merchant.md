# Session 18 — The Hemp Merchant

**Played:** 18 April 2026
**In-game date:** 33 Nachexen – 2 Jahrdrung 2512

## Summary

At the [[Pewter Merchant]], the party pressed [[Christina Asper]] about the murder and learned she had witnessed the killing — the "noble" was stabbed by the raider leader **Gustavus** during an argument over a young woman the raiders had expected to be handed over. Berin offered to stand as the innkeeper [[Anton Petz]]'s champion in a judicial duel. The party walked to the nearby **Grunwald farm** and freed **Herga**, a Nordland farm girl the Grunwald family had been holding with the intent to sell her as a servant — her account confirmed the "noble" was actually a commoner imposter and that the raiders hailed from the **Heidling farm** roughly fifteen miles north. They took a written affidavit from Herga, returned to the inn to a tearful [[Anton Petz|Petz]] who waived their room cost, and the next morning travelled east with [[Christina Asper|Asper]] toward the [[Hemp Merchant]]. On the road they passed the coroner at last making for the [[Pewter Merchant]]. At the Hemp Merchant they found an inn compound overrun with **Imperialist Engineer Pioneer Corps** and **[[Landsturm]]** soldiers upgrading the road and bridges north to **[[Norden]]** — far more activity than a simple infrastructure project. The proprietor **Jan Smuts** confirmed [[Professor Udo Stradovski|Stradovski]]'s passage on the 16th Nachexen, confirmed [[Elfrieda Teuschel]]'s group passed through just two nights ago paying with freshly minted crowns, and — most damning — identified the birthmark on the Pewter Merchant corpse as belonging to his own **servant boy**, who had vanished three or four days ago with a merchant named **Albrecht Felt**.

## Events

### Morning at the Pewter Merchant — Asper Confronted

- [[Boris Dorchen|Boris]] sat with [[Christina Asper]] and coaxed a rare, fleeting smile from her with one of his goofy stories
- [[Berin Garibaldi|Berin]] compared Asper unfavourably to **[[Condradine]]**, noting that both seemed to spend far too little time doing good for others — Asper's jaw tightened, her eyes narrowed, but she said nothing
- The desperate [[Anton Petz]] pleaded with the party to stay and help him make his case to the arriving coroner, fearing he would lose his inn
- [[Berin Garibaldi|Berin]] suggested a **judicial duel** and offered to stand as Petz's champion — the innkeeper accepted with trembling gratitude, though the prospect of involving Asper still filled him with visible dread
- [[Berin Garibaldi|Berin]] then confronted Asper directly — accused her of neglecting her duty, called her behaviour contemptible for a follower of [[Solkan]], and demanded she return the corrupt artefacts to be cleansed and destroyed at a proper settlement
- Asper dismissed every charge with icy calm — the murder was a **provincial matter** beneath her mission — but revealed what she had witnessed that night:
  - The raiders had expected the merchant to hand over **a young woman**
  - The merchant denied knowing them
  - The argument escalated; the raider leader **Gustavus** stabbed the man in the chest during a scuffle

### The Grunwald Farm — Herga

- The party set out on foot to the nearby **[[Grunwald Farm]]**, a reinforced imperial compound of walls and buildings grown haphazardly over the years, with a guard house and a bell pole at the entrance
- A heavily tanned, hunched farmer in his late forties answered the bell with suspicion — [[Hildegarde]]'s charm and the flash of her bounty hunter's badge got the party into the courtyard
- The farmer's wife eventually admitted they were holding a young woman and had intended to sell her as a servant come autumn — expecting to fetch **around six crowns** for her
- They found **[[Herga]]**, a girl from a Nordland family farm, kidnapped by raiders while gathering spring berries — bruises and scratches but otherwise unrestrained
- Herga's account:
  - The raiders, led by the violent **[[Gustavus]]**, had planned to sell her to the merchant at the inn
  - The merchant denied all knowledge of the arrangement, sparking the fatal argument
  - The merchant **had not spoken like a noble or educated man** — he sounded like someone from a local farm — deepening the party's suspicion that he was an impostor
  - The raiders were likely from the **[[Heidling Farm]]** about fifteen miles to the north, known for crossing the Nordland border to steal livestock and people alike
- When the farmer tried to raise his price from six to eight crowns, [[Hildegarde]] called his bluff — threatened to report the family for slavery and noted that a witch hunter was a short walk away
- [[Berin Garibaldi|Berin]] slowly loosened his sword in its scabbard and added that those who defied the laws of gods and men might find themselves answering to far less patient authorities
- The farmer raised his hands and released Herga **without payment**; [[Boris Dorchen|Boris]] quietly provided two gold crowns' worth of jewellery as a symbolic gesture

### Back at the Pewter Merchant — The Affidavit

- [[Hildegarde]] gave Herga a spare dagger for self-defence
- The party took a **formal written statement** emphasising:
  - The merchant's peasant-like speech
  - His failure to recognise the men he was supposedly meeting
  - The absence of any noble bearing or possessions on his person
- [[Anton Petz]] received the document with tearful gratitude and **waived the cost of the party's rooms**
- Petz prayed that Myrmidia would visit them in their time of need
- The party agreed Herga would travel with them to [[Ferlangen]] for refuge at the [[Temple of Shallya]] and eventual safe return home

### On the Road East

- [[Christina Asper]] roused the party at the **crack of dawn**; rolled her eyes at seeing Herga join the group, then mounted her horse without a word
- [[Berin Garibaldi|Berin]] spent the early hours teaching [[Hildegarde]] **basic combat drills and forms**
- Around midday they passed a group of road wardens escorting a **tall, thin, pasty-faced man on a gray nag** heading west — almost certainly the **coroner** finally making his way to the [[Pewter Merchant]]

### Arrival at the Hemp Merchant

- By evening the coast road met a well-kept track running north into the forest
- At the junction stood the **[[Hemp Merchant]]** inn — sign: a coil of rope over an anchor
- The compound was **swarming with Imperialist Engineer Pioneer Corps soldiers and [[Landsturm]] militia**, with supply wagons rumbling in and out of the northern track even as dusk settled
- [[Christina Asper]] dismounted, handed her reins to a visibly terrified stable boy, and walked away without a backward glance
- [[Berin Garibaldi|Berin]] stayed behind to help the overwhelmed boy improvise space for the party's **seven horses and wagon**, chatting him up in the process — confirming **[[Professor Udo Stradovski|Stradovski]] had stayed at the inn on the 16th Nachexen**, during a local founding celebration, travelling alone with the same brown horse bearing a white blaze

### Inside the Hemp Merchant — Soldiers and Intelligence

- Proprietor **[[Jan Smuts]]** was run ragged, barely had time to breathe
- [[Christina Asper|Asper]] somehow secured a quiet corner table just by approaching it — the soldiers there apparently deciding they had somewhere else to be
- [[Boris Dorchen|Boris]] approached the officers' table to ask about the military activity and was immediately told to shoo
- [[Pilpoul Ivri|Pilpoul]] launched a loud, mock-indignant rant scolding Boris for harassing the hard-working soldiers — while doing so he glimpsed the **blueprints** spread across the officers' table: detailed designs for **bridges and road sections**, all pointing north
- [[Pilpoul Ivri|Pilpoul]] then joined a group of senior enlisted men and learned:
  - The **Imperialist Engineer Pioneer Corps** had been ordered to **upgrade the road and bridges all the way to [[Norden]]**
  - Built strong enough to support the **heaviest wagons**
  - Working **day and night** from both ends of the route
- [[Berin Garibaldi|Berin]] approached the officers as a fellow man of arms and confirmed:
  - No unusual disease outbreaks among the troops
  - The officers were aware of Asper and unconcerned
- Scale indicators:
  - Artisans and engineers called up from across the Empire under **direct Imperial orders**
  - [[Landsturm]] insignia mean a year of lost taxes per man — enormously expensive
  - This is something far larger than a simple road improvement

### Jan Smuts — Stradovski, Elfrieda, and the Servant Boy

After the soldiers retired and the great room quieted, the party cornered **[[Jan Smuts]]**:

- Confirmed a man matching [[Professor Udo Stradovski|Stradovski]]'s description passed through about **two weeks prior**, heading east with a haughty Middenland accent, travelling alone
- Confirmed a **dark-haired noblewoman** had stayed **just two nights ago** with one large, burly man, paying with a **freshly minted crown** bearing the same illegal markings (Elfrieda's group)
- Boris described the **birthmark** of the Pewter Merchant murder victim — Smuts went pale: the mark belonged to **his own servant boy**, who had vanished three or four days ago at the same time as a merchant named **[[Albrecht Felt]]**, having **helped the man onto his horse before disappearing entirely**
- Smuts was far too overwhelmed to send anyone to identify the body; [[Pilpoul Ivri|Pilpoul]] offered to help draft a letter to be carried back by the next westbound traveller
- [[Hildegarde]] quietly purchased an apple from the innkeeper before the long night closed
