# Session 03 — The Trial

## Summary
The session opened with Pilpoul losing a gold crown to an improbable series of ricochets — the coin finding its way to a stranger who used it to pay off his mortgage. The party attended [[Klaus Grebel]]'s heresy trial at [[Fulpfasser Court]], where [[Pilpoul]] effectively acted as defence barrister alongside volunteer lawyer [[Konrad Stellmann]]. Klaus was found guilty but escaped execution in favour of community service under the [[Cult of Shallya]]. Afterwards, [[Elke Grebel]] took the party to the [[Clerk's Office (Verena-Ulric)]] where they enrolled as provisional [[Middenland Militia]] members, received advance pay, and were briefed on multiple leads for finding [[Professor Udo Stradovski]].

## Events

### The Lost Gold Crown
As the party exited the [[Commission for Trade Commerce and Taxation]], a portly merchant pushed past [[Pilpoul]], causing a gold crown to fall and bounce improbably down the stairs, through the streets, and land at the feet of a downcast stranger. The stranger used the coin to pay off his mortgage at a banker's. [[Boris]] attributed this to Shallya's judgment for [[Pilpoul]] having spoken her name in vain; Pilpoul insisted it had been reverence. The coin was not recovered.

### Trial of Klaus Grebel
At [[Fulpfasser Court]], the party met [[Konrad Stellmann]], the nervous pro bono defence lawyer. [[Pilpoul]] coached him before the trial: guilt lies with intent, not interpretation (Article 12 of the Codex Civis Middenheimenis).

**Prosecution** (led by [[Danderich Flisshausten]], Knight of the [[Ordo Sancti Inquisita]])
- Charged Klaus with the Heresy of Arctus of Carolberg (condemned 1265) — asserting Sigmar was not noble-born, thereby undermining the divine basis of nobility
- Witnesses: [[Hermann Wurt]] (eyewitness) and [[Heinricus Ralf]] (Sigmarite theological expert)

**Defence (Pilpoul as effective barrister)**
- Cross-examined [[Hermann Wurt]]: challenged his distance from Klaus, crowd noise, lack of written notes, and his stated intent to intercept Klaus (not a neutral witness). Called his testimony "Herman worthless" — reprimanded and fined 5 shillings, sixpence by Judge Verner. Subsequently incited the gallery to murmur in protest until the judge threatened to clear the courtroom.
- Cross-examined [[Heinricus Ralf]]: revealed he was not present at the plaza; relied on affidavits. Raised the Rule of Pre-Trial Disclosure — affidavits had not been provided in advance. Judge allowed a 10-minute recess. During the recess, [[Pilpoul]] noted all affidavits appeared written in the same hand with different signatures and unnaturally uniform phrasing — suggesting fabrication or collusion.
- Defence witnesses: [[Hildegarde]] (testified she was directly in front of Klaus; described his speech as anti-guild, citing Ulric, speaking favourably of Sigmar and the government; identified red-clad ruffians as the aggressors who fled when guards arrived) and [[Boris]] (testified he exited the tavern to find Klaus already under attack by "vicious Sigmarites"; mentioned his father's saying that Sigmar was an Ulrican; confirmed under cross that no ruffians were arrested and no guards took his testimony).
- Argued: independent, non-colluded defence testimony vs. suspiciously uniform prosecution affidavits; the Watch failed to investigate the ruffians (investigative malpractice).

**Verdict** — [[Judge Magdalyn Verner]]: Guilty of speaking heresy as charged, but — in consideration of the defence's arguments about intent, Klaus's use of Sigmar as an example against guild cartels rather than a doctrinal statement, his youth and sincerity, and the quality of the defence — sentenced to **community service under the [[Cult of Shallya]], minimum 3 months**. No execution. No long-term imprisonment.

**After the verdict:** [[Pilpoul]] thanked the judge and shook [[Danderich Flisshausten]]'s hand — who crushed it for several seconds and warned Pilpoul not to cross paths again. [[Konrad Stellmann]] was delighted. [[Elke Grebel]] ran out and hugged each party member; confirmed Klaus is back in the church and the Inquisition cannot kill him.

### Enrolling in the Militia
[[Elke Grebel]] took the party to the [[Clerk's Office (Verena-Ulric)]] — a grand building bearing the scales of Verena and the wolf of Ulric. A clerk processed the warrant for [[Find Professor Stradovski]].

Party registrations:
- [[Boris]] — "hunter"
- [[Altus]] — "mercenary protecting merchants and caravans"
- [[Hildegarde]] — "no paid occupation"
- [[Pilpoul]] — "autodidact"
- [[Joerg Wusterburg]] — "novice in the Order of the Howling Wolf"

All gave physical descriptions. All swore fealty to the [[Middenland Militia]]: loyalty, honour, defence of city and people, uphold laws, obey lawful commands, service until honourable discharge or death.

**Pay:** 6 Gold Crowns advance per member (fortnight's pay). Standard rate: 8s/day; specialist: 10s/day. Each received a militia pass with future payment dates — losing it means losing pay.

**Equipment:** [[Altus]] took the militia kit (crossbow, mail shirt, shield, sword) for a 5 GC deposit.

**Provisions:** A wagon of provisions available if the party needs to leave the city.
