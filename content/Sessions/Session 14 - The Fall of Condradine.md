# Session 14 — The Fall of Condradine

**Played:** 14 March 2026
**In-game date:** 29–30 Nachexen 2512 (night)

## Summary

The [[Forest Inn]] revealed its true horror during the night. [[Falconius Thorncobble|Connie]]'s midnight foraging in the kitchen uncovered a room of shocking neglect and rot, and a trapdoor in the floor led to a cellar packed with meticulously stripped animal bones — no rats, no scraps, no food, only bare bones. Simultaneously, [[Boris Dorchen]] heard suspicious sounds from [[Hoffman Jannitzer|Hoffman]]'s room and broke down the door with his axe — only to find [[Condradine]] calmly overseeing the lowering of an unconscious [[Hoffman Jannitzer]] out of the window on a rope, toward a group of pale, emaciated ghoulish figures waiting below with a flatbed hand cart. [[Hildegarde]] shot [[Condradine]] with her crossbow, but [[Condradine]] leapt for the rope and fell the full height of the building to her death. Despite desperate fighting from multiple party members, the ghoulish figures managed to wheel the unconscious [[Hoffman Jannitzer]] through the inn's main gate and into the night before the party could stop them.

## Key Events

### The Night Watch Begins

- The inn's unusually thick wooden walls — nearly a foot wide — swallowed all sound between chambers
- [[Berin Garibaldi|Berin]] took up a meditative vigil in the stables: kneeling before his makeshift altar, sword across his knees, plate helm fitted close, watching through the night
- [[Falconius Thorncobble|Connie]] and [[Rolf]] kept watch in the corridor
- [[Hildegarde]] prepared to relieve them later; [[Pilpoul Ivri]] retired to bed, to be roused before dawn
- Rain pattered against the windows and then stopped, leaving the inn in perfect, unsettling silence

### Connie's Kitchen Investigation

- [[Falconius Thorncobble|Connie]], unable to find so much as a crust in the corridor, led [[Rolf]] quietly to the kitchen to scavenge something edible
- The kitchen was in a state of shocking neglect: stone floor crusted with layers of rotting food, pots and bowls baked with old sauces, shelves lined with barrels and crates that yielded only worm-riddled apples and rubbery foul-smelling carrots
- Even [[Rolf]], who would normally bury his nose in every scrap, pulled back from the scents with visible distaste
- There was no meat, no hanging poultry — nothing suggesting a kitchen capable of feeding paying guests

### The Trapdoor and the Bone Cellar

- [[Hildegarde]] joined [[Falconius Thorncobble|Connie]] in the kitchen after checking on [[Berin Garibaldi|Berin]] in the stables; [[Berin Garibaldi|Berin]] shared [[Mungo Mudfoot]]'s bread with the famished [[Falconius Thorncobble|Connie]]
- [[Hildegarde]] quietly unlatched the kitchen's back door as a precaution
- While searching side storage, they noticed a **trapdoor set into the floor**
- [[Berin Garibaldi|Berin]] descended first, candle-light held close by [[Hildegarde]], shield raised and sword ready
- Below: not a cold store of provisions but a **root cellar packed with bones** — piles and piles of them, white and utterly clean, stripped of every scrap of meat and gristle by careful knife work rather than animal teeth
- Butcher hooks hung from the rafters above; not a single rat stirred in the darkness
- [[Berin Garibaldi|Berin]] confirmed the bones were from cattle and fowl — consumed with a thoroughness suggesting someone had survived on them through desperate necessity while leaving the vegetables above to rot

### Boris Hears Something (Simultaneously)

- While the others were below, [[Boris Dorchen]] was listening at the wall near [[Hoffman Jannitzer|Hoffman]]'s room upstairs
- He heard: a thump, the creak of something opening, and the low murmur of voices within
- He threw himself against the door — found it barricaded from the inside; his own room key failed to fit the heavier lock
- [[Boris Dorchen]] began **hacking through the door with his axe**
- The noise woke [[Pilpoul Ivri]], who stumbled into the hallway in his sleeping clothes, bewildered
- [[Falconius Thorncobble|Connie]] spotted movement through the great room windows: three to five figures crossing the courtyard in the dead of night
- The party barred the kitchen's back door and moved to secure the inn; [[Hildegarde]] sprinted upstairs to raise the alarm

### Condradine's Operation Exposed

- When [[Boris Dorchen]] and [[Hildegarde]] finally broke through the top half of [[Hoffman Jannitzer|Hoffman]]'s door, they found [[Condradine]] inside — calmly overseeing the lowering of an unconscious [[Hoffman Jannitzer]] out of the window by rope
- [[Hoffman Jannitzer|Hoffman]] hung limp, bound under the arms, being guided down toward the courtyard where [[Gunter]] and four other figures — pale, sunken-cheeked, dressed in tatters — waited to receive him onto a flatbed hand cart
- [[Hildegarde]] fired her crossbow and caught [[Condradine]] in the leg; the woman barely flinched
- In a desperate bid to escape, [[Condradine]] leapt from the window toward the rope — and missed
- **[[Condradine]] fell the full height of the building**, struck the edge of the cart with her head, and hit the ground dead

### The Battle in the Courtyard

- The ghoulish figures below spotted [[Falconius Thorncobble|Connie]] at the window and panicked, dropping [[Hoffman Jannitzer|Hoffman]] onto the cart and moving to flee
- [[Boris Dorchen]] and [[Pilpoul Ivri]] fired from the upper windows; bolts and arrows found their marks among the pale, emaciated figures
- [[Falconius Thorncobble|Connie]] pelted the ghouls with his sling from above, cracking one man's arm with a well-placed stone
- [[Berin Garibaldi|Berin]], alone in the courtyard, called out to [[Orazio]] in the language of their bond; his warhorse moved from the stable toward him
- [[Berin Garibaldi|Berin]] disengaged from two creatures clawing at him with elongated, sharpened nails, hauled himself into the saddle, and drove [[Orazio]] forward in a charge — hooves crashing into the kidnappers
- [[Hildegarde]] dropped her crossbow, drew sword and shield, and sprinted for the gate to support him

### Hoffman Is Taken

- Despite the wounds dealt and chaos unleashed, the remaining ghoulish figures did not stop
- One sliced the rope free, another took the handles of the cart, and together they pushed [[Hoffman Jannitzer]] through the inn's main gate and out into the dark road beyond
- The party gave chase as best they could, but the night swallowed the cart and its ghoulish escort — leaving the courtyard strewn with the fallen and the sound of retreating wheels fading into silence
- **[[Hoffman Jannitzer]] was successfully abducted**; the party had been unable to prevent it

- [[Condradine]] was not a traveler — she was an operative whose mission was to drug and abduct [[Hoffman Jannitzer]]
- The kitchen bone cellar confirms the inn has been subsisting entirely on animal flesh with meticulous butchery — the vegetables above were left to rot while every scrap of meat was consumed; the complete absence of rats in the cellar is notable
- The ghoulish figures are described as pale, emaciated, with elongated and sharpened nails — human in origin, but clearly transformed
- [[Hoffman Jannitzer]] is now a captive of whatever force operates through the [[Forest Inn]]
- [[Condradine]]'s death was accidental — she missed the rope
