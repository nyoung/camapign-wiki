# Session 17 — Asper-ated

**Played:** 11 April 2026
**In-game date:** 32 Nachexen 2512

## Summary

The party arrived at the [[Royal Wolf Inn|Royal Wolf]], a fortified inn and militia compound just south of the village of [[Schoten]] in [[Ostland]]. After clearing a militia interrogation (they were suspected of being Nordland spies), [[Berin Garibaldi|Berin]] surrendered the Khainite tome that had been weighing on his conscience to the militia. Inside the common room they met [[Christina Asper]], a strikingly young Solkanite witch hunter dispatched from the University of Altdorf to assist in the pursuit of [[Professor Udo Stradovski]]. [[Pilpoul Ivri|Pilpoul]] promptly accused her of complicity in Stradovski's escape, was talked down, and the party exchanged intelligence with her — Asper in turn demanded and received [[Hildegarde]]'s Khainite-marked crossbow and black mask. The next day the party travelled east in Asper's company, caught their first glimpse of the [[Sea of Claws]], and arrived at the [[Pewter Merchant]] inn, where a nervous innkeeper named [[Anton Petz]] was facing a ruinous hundred-crown fine for a "noble" stabbed in his establishment. The body turned out to be a commoner in ill-fitting noble's clothing — a man who had presented himself as the merchant "Masten Feltt." A witch hunter had also been present at the inn the night of the killing. That witch hunter was Christina Asper.

## Key Events

### Arrival at the Royal Wolf

- [[Royal Wolf Inn|The Royal Wolf]] revealed in full: an earthen rampart and ditches enclosing a squat stone tower, a new barracks with firing slits, and a toll house — all flying the freshly painted [[Ostland]] bull
- A militia sergeant interrogated the party on suspicion of being Nordland spies before clearing them
- Wagon and animals stabled; party entered the inn proper
- In a quiet corner before entering, [[Berin Garibaldi|Berin]] and [[Pilpoul Ivri|Pilpoul]] discussed the Khainite tome that had weighed on Berin's conscience throughout the journey — Berin handed the dark book over to the militia, eager to be rid of its burden

### Meeting Christina Asper

- A solitary figure in the dark vestments of a witch hunter sat in the far corner of the common room, sipping spiced wine, studying the newcomers coolly
- [[Hoffman Jannitzer]] recognised her immediately: **[[Christina Asper]]**, sent by the **University of Altdorf** to assist in the pursuit of [[Professor Udo Stradovski|Stradovski]]
- Strikingly young — perhaps mid-twenties — wide-brimmed hat perfectly starched into a flawless circle, silver fist entwined with serpents (the symbol of [[Solkan]]) gleaming on her chest
- The party surmised that someone so young achieving full witch hunter rank had done so through exceptional ability alone

### Pilpoul's Ill-Advised Accusation

- In a bold and reckless moment, [[Pilpoul Ivri|Pilpoul]] accused Asper **herself** of being connected to [[Professor Udo Stradovski|Stradovski]]'s escape, citing her youth and improbable rank
- [[Hoffman Jannitzer]] went pale and frantically mouthed for Pilpoul to stop
- [[Boris Dorchen|Boris]] took two deliberate steps back, pointed at Pilpoul, and declared he had never seen the man before in his life
- Pilpoul hastily withdrew the accusation with elaborate apologies; [[Hildegarde]] smoothed things over by explaining the long road had taught the party to question everything
- Asper fixed the table with a steely glare but eventually gestured for them to sit

### Exchange of Intelligence

Asper explained the University's position:
- Only a professor expecting execution would flee a **Verenan trial**, where a miracle of truth-telling could compel confession of innermost beliefs
- She believed Stradovski was aligned with one of the ruinous powers, though in what aspect remained unclear

The party laid out their findings:
- Stradovski's connection to the Khainite priest **[[Melmoth]]**
- The anatomical horrors at [[Beekerhoven]]
- The body-snatching ring
- The illegal freshly-minted coins linking the conspiracy to a wider network
- [[Berin Garibaldi|Berin]] placed the dark sword taken from [[Melmoth]] and the Khaine tome on the table; Asper covered them with her leather satchel and recorded every detail in her small journal with handwriting so precise it looked printed by a press

Asper's own intelligence:
- A noblewoman named **[[Elfrieda Teuschel]]** was believed to be funding [[Professor Udo Stradovski|Stradovski]]'s activities
- Another witch hunter, **[[Lucas Pollack]]**, had been left behind in [[Ferlangen]] to track the professor's movements through the region
- A small group matching Elfrieda's description — a dark-haired woman and a large male servant — had stayed at the [[Royal Wolf Inn|Royal Wolf]] **just two nights prior**

### Surrender of the Khainite Items

- Asper turned her gaze to the Khainite items the party was openly carrying: [[Hildegarde]]'s crossbow bearing the enemy gods' marks, and the black mask she wore over her scar
- Asper demanded they be surrendered
- [[Hildegarde]] considered it in silence for a long moment, then quietly handed them over — reasoning that whatever her personal feelings, Asper was the authority on such matters
- Throughout the exchange, Hildegarde and Asper engaged in a prolonged, wordless battle of wills — neither willing to look away first

### The Evening at the Royal Wolf

- [[Hildegarde]] performed for the soldiers in the common room — cartwheels and juggling **[[Rolf]]** — and negotiated a two-shilling rebate on the rooms
- [[Berin Garibaldi|Berin]] sought a sparring partner; finding only the guards on duty, [[Boris Dorchen|Boris]] offered to spar with him
- [[Hildegarde]] quietly asked about a kidnapped farm girl from [[Nordland]] — the Ostland soldiers had no interest (in their view the girl had simply chosen the winning side)
- Asper was pounding on doors and kicking benches at first light, declaring that daylight was burning
- Before setting out, [[Berin Garibaldi|Berin]] trained with the militia commander, trading sword work against the captain's cavalry hammer — the captain was forthcoming about the quality of the roads between the Royal Wolf and [[Ferlangen]] but went tight-lipped on provincial governance or military matters

### On the Road to the Pewter Merchant

- [[Falconius Thorncobble|Connie]] calculated the four-day journey to [[Ferlangen]] would require at least **twelve pies**
- [[Mungo Mudfoot]] entertained [[Boris Dorchen|Boris]] for the better part of the day with an extraordinarily detailed drawing he called a **"pie chart"** — a meticulous illustration of various meat and beet pies rendered with loving care
- [[Pilpoul Ivri|Pilpoul]] spent much of the travel drawing [[Christina Asper]] out on her Solkanite beliefs; she spoke at length about discipline, purity, and perfection; described her role not as a judge but as an **executioner**; her worldview was starkly black and white
- The road climbed out of the forest and the iron-grey expanse of the [[Sea of Claws]] appeared on the horizon — most of the party were seeing a body of water that stretched beyond sight for the first time, and they fell briefly silent

### Arrival at the Pewter Merchant

- [[Pewter Merchant|The Pewter Merchant]] — a tidy inn where the road met the coast
- Innkeeper **[[Anton Petz]]** greeted them with a clean apron and a courtyard in good order, but his hands never stopped twisting and untwisting the corners of a towel, and his eyes kept drifting toward a particular spot near the hearth
- **[[Rolf]]** immediately began nuzzling at that same spot — a section of floor scrubbed so aggressively the varnish was gone and fresh wood showed beneath
- The dog then tracked a scent toward a storeroom at the back, where a road warden sat in a posture that suggested guarding rather than resting

### The Body in the Storeroom

- [[Falconius Thorncobble|Connie]] approached [[Anton Petz]] about the ratter's scent; Petz pulled the group aside in barely contained distress
- Several days ago a fight had broken out between a noble and a group of **reivers** from the north, resulting in the noble's death
- Under [[Ostland]] law, the location where a noble is slain is subject to a fine of **one hundred crowns** — enough to bankrupt Petz and cost him the inn
- [[Christina Asper|Asper]] made clear she had no intention of being drawn into a side matter
- The road warden (roused from a half-asleep state) described the fight as sudden and chaotic — raised voices, a knife, and the noble was dead before anyone could intervene; she also mentioned, almost in passing, that a **young redhead girl** had been present just before the violence

### Examining the "Noble"

The deceased was a man in his mid-thirties to early forties, dressed in a fine tunic, expensive pants, and boots dyed a rich purple. At first glance a wealthy man — but closer inspection told a different story:

- Clothing did not fit — tunic too short, vest too long, boots far too large with the toes sitting several centimeters from the end
- Hands calloused, fingernails dirty and unkempt
- Lice moved through his hair
- [[Pilpoul Ivri|Pilpoul]] laid it out: this was a **commoner who had assumed a noble's identity**, likely walking unknowingly into a trap set for the man whose clothes he wore
- The road warden confirmed the deceased had presented himself as the honorable merchant **[[Masten Feltt]]** and had arrived on horseback with **no luggage whatsoever** — a detail that, in hindsight, should have raised suspicion immediately

### Stradovski's Trail, Confirmed

- [[Hildegarde]] produced the illegal newly-minted penny the party had been tracking and asked whether the deceased had paid with similar coins
- [[Anton Petz|Petz]] shook his head — but fished beneath the desk and produced a **freshly minted gold crown** with the same suspicious markings, used by a distinguished gentleman who had passed through **approximately twelve to fourteen days prior**
- [[Hildegarde]] showed the wanted poster of [[Professor Udo Stradovski|Stradovski]]; Petz confirmed the man matched the description
- [[Professor Udo Stradovski|Stradovski]] had travelled alone on horseback, paid for a single night, eaten a meal, and departed at first light without a word about his destination
- [[Hildegarde]] pressed a shilling into Petz's hand as a gesture of goodwill — both of them knew it barely made a dent in the hundred-crown problem hanging over him

### Boris and Asper, Over Dinner

- [[Boris Dorchen|Boris]] took the opportunity to sit across from [[Christina Asper]] and praise her focus and integrity, commiserating about the party's tendency to get sidetracked by every stray injustice, and laying the blame squarely at [[Hoffman Jannitzer|Hoffman]]'s feet
- Asper made no effort to hide her low opinion of the group as a whole, but found Boris to be the **most tolerable** among them
- Boris was quietly pleased with himself, having found the one approach that seemed to crack her composure — **simply agreeing with everything she already believed**

### The Revelation

- As an afterthought, the road warden mentioned that a **witch hunter** had also been present at the [[Pewter Merchant]] on the night the supposed noble was killed
- The party absorbed this in silence
- [[Christina Asper|Asper]] — their new ally, their meticulous note-taker, their self-proclaimed instrument of pure and focused justice — had been at this very inn on the night a man was stabbed to death under suspicious circumstances
- No one had yet confronted her. The question of what she knew, and what she had done or not done, remained unanswered as the night drew in around the [[Pewter Merchant]]
