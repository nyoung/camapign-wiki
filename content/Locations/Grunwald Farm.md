# Grunwald Farm

## Description
A reinforced imperial compound of walls and buildings that have grown haphazardly over the years, with a guard house and a bell pole at the entrance. Located within walking distance of the [[Pewter Merchant]]. The kind of farmstead that pays its taxes and is otherwise left alone.

## Notable Features
- Defensive walls and guard house
- Bell pole at the entrance for visitors to ring
- A haphazard mix of buildings accumulated over years
- The Grunwald family held [[Herga]] here, intending to sell her as a servant come autumn

## Notable NPCs
- An unnamed **heavily tanned, hunched farmer** in his late forties — opened the gate with suspicion; tried to raise his asking price for Herga from six to eight crowns before relenting under pressure
- An unnamed **farmer's wife** — admitted to holding Herga

## Factions Present
- None on the record — a private family farm

## Connected Locations
- [[Pewter Merchant]] — within walking distance

## History
- **[[Session 18 - The Hemp Merchant|Session 18]]:** Party walked here on foot from the [[Pewter Merchant]]. [[Hildegarde]]'s charm and bounty-hunter's badge got them through the gate. The wife admitted they were holding a young woman; the farmer tried to bump the price. [[Hildegarde]] invoked slavery laws and the nearby witch hunter; [[Berin Garibaldi|Berin]] loosened his sword in its scabbard. [[Herga]] was released without payment. [[Boris Dorchen|Boris]] quietly provided two gold crowns' worth of jewellery as a symbolic gesture.

## Notes
- The family's willingness to hold and sell a kidnapped girl is a pattern of behaviour consistent with the Ostland/Nordland border culture — fits with [[Session 17 - Asper-ated|Session 17]]'s earlier suggestion that the Ostland militia had no interest in a Nordland girl because "she had chosen the winning side"
