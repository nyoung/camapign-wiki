# Pewter Merchant

## Description
A tidy coaching inn where the road east from the [[Royal Wolf Inn]] meets the [[Ostland]] coast and the first iron-grey expanse of the [[Sea of Claws]]. Proprietor [[Anton Petz]] keeps a clean apron and a courtyard in good order — but in [[Session 17 - Asper-ated|Session 17]] his nerves were visibly shredded by the body in his storeroom and the ruinous hundred-crown fine it had put over his head.

## Notable Features
- Coastal position with views of the [[Sea of Claws]]
- Storeroom at the back where the dead "merchant" is being kept under road-warden guard
- A scrubbed section of floor near the hearth — the varnish worn through to bare wood where blood was cleaned away

## Notable NPCs
- [[Anton Petz]] — proprietor, nervous wreck
- Unnamed road warden (female) — guarding the body; eyewitness to the killing
- [[Christina Asper]] — was present at the inn the night of the killing and did not intervene

## Factions Present
- Ostland road wardens (one warden present)

- [[Ferlangen]] — further east, the party's destination

## History
- **[[Session 17 - Asper-ated|Session 17]]:** Party arrived with [[Christina Asper]]. [[Rolf]] tracked blood near the hearth and a scent toward the storeroom. [[Anton Petz]] explained the crisis: several days prior a fight had broken out between a "noble" and a group of **reivers** from the north, resulting in the noble's death; under Ostland law a noble slain on the premises means a hundred-crown fine. The party examined the body and identified the "noble" as a commoner impostor who had presented himself as the merchant [[Masten Feltt]]. [[Hildegarde]] produced the illegal penny; Petz produced a freshly minted gold crown from a "distinguished gentleman" matching [[Professor Udo Stradovski|Stradovski]]'s description who had passed through 12–14 days prior. The road warden then mentioned — almost in passing — that a witch hunter had also been at the inn the night of the killing. That witch hunter was Christina Asper.
- **[[Session 18 - The Hemp Merchant|Session 18]]:** [[Christina Asper]] admitted she witnessed the killing — raider leader **[[Gustavus]]** stabbed the "merchant" in a scuffle after the man denied the raiders' prior arrangement to buy a young woman. [[Berin Garibaldi|Berin]] offered to stand as Petz's champion in a judicial duel and confronted Asper on her duty. Party walked to the **[[Grunwald Farm]]** and freed **[[Herga]]** — the kidnapped girl — and took her statement. Petz received the affidavit with tearful gratitude and waived the cost of the rooms. Party departed east at dawn with Asper and Herga; coroner passed them heading west on the road.

## Notes
- [[Stradovski trail]]: confirmed passage here ~12–14 days before Session 17
- The hundred-crown fine is effectively solvable: the dead man has been identified as the [[Hemp Merchant]]'s missing servant boy (per [[Jan Smuts]], Session 18), not a noble — the party holds an affidavit from [[Herga]] and the coroner is on his way
- The "redhead girl" present before the killing was **[[Herga]]** — now located and travelling with the party
