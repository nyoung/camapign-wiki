# Temple to Liadriel

## Description
Part inn, part temple, built by the current innkeeper's great-grandfather as a meeting place between humans and elves for trade and the sharing of ideas. Dedicated to Liadriel, the Elven goddess of song and wine — reputedly the only such temple in existence. The building features human stonework carved with elven motifs: grapevines, wine-making, and musical instruments; the script resembles, but is not genuine, elvish writing. The interior is festooned with garlands of wildflowers hung from the beams.

## Notable Features
- Human stonework with carved elven motifs: grapevines, wine-making, musical instruments
- Script resembling but not genuinely elvish
- Interior decorated with garlands of wildflowers on the beams
- A faded wooden sign nearby points north toward [[Neu-Emskronk]]
- Nearby stone foundations mark where a village once stood

## Notable NPCs
- Tall, thin innkeeper — great-grandson of the temple's founder; explained the history and current estrangement from the elves

## Events
- **[[Session 13 - Where the Wild Things Aren't|Session 13]]:** Party stopped here. The innkeeper explained: elves have no tradition of building temples, preferring song, dance, and festival; trade with the elves dried up when the Baron of Nordland made aggressive moves toward their forest, causing the elves to retreat and sever contact; travellers have gone missing between inns for years; road wardens bring mutant bodies through periodically.

## Notes
- The temple's origin reflects a now-lost tradition of human-elf cooperation in Nordland, severed by the current Baron's hostility toward the [[Laurelorn Forest]]
